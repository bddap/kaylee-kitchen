The Joy of Cooking Scones

From the kitchen of

Dana Dirksen

serves
ready in
Sift together in a large bowl:
1 3/4 cups sifted all-purpose flour 2 teaspoons baking powder
1 tablespoon sugar
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/4 cup cold butter
2 eggs beaten (reserving 2 Tbsp for spreading on the scones before baking)
1/3 cup cream
1/3 cup raisins, currants or cranberries (optional)
Steps

1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.

2. Sift together in a large bowl, the flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt.

3. Cut into these ingredients, until the size of small peas, the butter.

4. Add dry fruits. (optional)

5. Make a well in the dry ingredients. Pour the liq- uid into it. Combine with a few swift strokes. Han- dle the dough as little as possible, or it will be tough.

6. Place on a lightly floured board. Pat into a 3/4-

1 inch thick circle. Cut into 2 inch rounds or pie shaped wedges. Brush with the reserved beaten egg and sprinkle with sugar.

7. Place on a greased or nonstick baking sheet and bake for about 15 minutes or until golden brown. Makes 12 scones. Serve with whipped unsalted but- ter and jam.
