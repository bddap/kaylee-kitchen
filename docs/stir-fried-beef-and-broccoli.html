Stir-Fried Beef and Broccoli

From the kitchen of

Dana Dirksen

serves
ready in
Beef:
2 tsp soy sauce
1/4 tsp sugar
1/4 tsp salt
3/4 pound boneless sirloin, but across the grain into 1/4‚Äù thick slices
Sauce:
1 Tbsp cornstarch
1 Tbsp soy sauce
1 Tbsp medium-dry Sherry or Scotch
1/4 cup chicken or beef broth or water
1 tsp sugar
2 tsp Oriental sesame oil
3 Tbsp vegetable oil
1 Tbsp minced peeled fresh gingerroot
1 Tbsp minced garlic
a 4-inch fresh red chili, seeded and minced (wearing gloves)
1 lb broccoli, cut into flowerets and the stems removed and cut into 1/2 inch thick sticks
Steps

Prepare beef:

In a small bowl stir together the soy sauce, sugar, salt and beef and let it marinate for 20 minutes.

Make the sauce while the beef is marinating:

In a small bowl dissolve the cornstarch in the soy sauce and stir in the Sherry, the broth, the sugar, and the sesa- me oil.

Cook meat and broccoli:

Heat wok or large heavy skillet over high heat until it

is hot, add 2 Tbsp of the vegetable oil, and heat it un-

til it just begins to smoke. Stir-fry the beef in the oil in small batches for 1 minute, or until it is no longer pink, and transfer it as it is cooked with a slotted spoon to a plate. Add the remaining 1 Tbsp of vegatable oil to the wok, heat it until it is hot but not smoking, and in the

oil stir-fry the gingerroot, the garlic, and the chili for 30 seconds, or until the mixture is fragrant. Add the broc- coli and stir-fry the mixture for 1 minute. Add 1/3 cup water and steam the broccoli, covered, for 1 to 2 min. Stir in sauce, add it to the wok with the beef and any broth that has accumulated on the plate, and cook the mixture, stirring for 2 minute, or until the sauce is thickened and the beef is heated through.

Serve with rice. Makes 2 servings.
